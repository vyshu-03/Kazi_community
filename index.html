History

The Kazi (or Qazi) community has historically been associated with the role of judges in Islamic societies. Kazis were responsible for interpreting and administering Islamic law (Sharia) in civil, criminal, and religious matters. This role made them influential figures in their communities, entrusted with ensuring justice and guiding ethical conduct.

Famous People

Qazi Motahar Hossain

Qazi Motahar Hossain was a renowned Bangladeshi writer, statistician, and academician. He made substantial contributions to literature, science, and education in Bangladesh.

Syed Mahmood

Syed Mahmood was a distinguished judge of the Allahabad High Court in the late 19th century. He was one of the first Indian judges in British India and made significant contributions to the development of legal thought and jurisprudence in India.

Qazi Muhammad Essa

Qazi Muhammad Essa was a prominent figure in the Pakistan Movement. He played a crucial role in the struggle for the independence of Pakistan and was a close associate of Muhammad Ali Jinnah.
